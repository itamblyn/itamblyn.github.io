  • Graduate students

    Students interested in Graduate Studies in Physics at the University of Ottawa at the MSc and PhD level.

    Example courses: Physics and Chemistry of Materials, Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics, Theory of the Solid State, Surface Science, Experimental Techniques, Synthetic Techniques, Computational Chemistry.

    For more information on the programs see here:

    • MSc in Physics at uOttawa
    • PhD in Physics at uOttawa

    Within CLEAN, we are always looking for talented individuals to join our team. See our     Research page for further information. Although computational background is not necessary, it is a plus. If you are interested in joining our research team, please contact me (isaac.tamblyn@nrc-cnrc.gc.ca) with a cover letter and CV outlining your interests and experience. Please include contact information for three references, and note whether you are an international or a domestic (Canadian citizen/ permanent resident) candidate as this can have an impact on funding.

    There are several scholarship opportunities for both Canadian (including Permanent Residents) and international candidates. Students who hold OGS Scholarships from the Ontario Provincial Government, or NSERC PGS awards from the Canadian federal government, are eligible for top-up Awards.

  • Undergraduate students


    Working in our research group is an excellent way to gain valuable researach and computing experience. Undergraduate students wishing to work in our group are encouraged to contact me. The following funding opportunities exist for undergraduate students in our group:

    NSERC Undergraduate Student Research Award (USRA)
    NSERC Industrial USRA
    MITACS Globalink (for international students from India, Brazil, China and Mexico)

  • Postdoctoral researchers
